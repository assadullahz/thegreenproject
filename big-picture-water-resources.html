Indonesia's Water Revolution

$7.2 Billion Investment for 85% Urban Wastewater Treatment & 2,000 GWh Energy Generation

In addressing critical water challenges, Indonesia is spearheading a comprehensive approach encompassing a $7.2 billion investment, advanced technologies, and ambitious quantitative goals to revolutionize water treatment, sanitation, and energy generation from wastewater.

Investment Focus

Indonesia is committed to addressing water treatment and wastewater challenges through a targeted investment of $7.2 billion in water and sanitation infrastructure by 2025.

Access Improvement

The government aims to improve access to clean water by implementing measures to treat 85% of urban wastewater, ensuring a more sustainable and sanitary environment.

Technological Advancements

Indonesia is embracing advanced technologies in wastewater treatment, showcasing a commitment to innovation and efficiency in managing water resources.

Energy Generation

A key highlight is the goal to generate 2,000 gigawatt-hours of energy from wastewater treatment processes, contributing significantly to the nation's renewable energy portfolio.

Sustainability Milestones

With quantifiable benchmarks and innovative solutions, Indonesia's approach to water treatment aligns with broader sustainability goals, making strides towards a resilient and eco-friendly future.

Existing Wastewater Management Issues

Wastewater management in Indonesia is still far from its ideal state due to planning policy and treatments that has not efficiently manage wastewater. If remain untreated, losses will exceed monetary value, but it will raise health issues and labor productivity.
