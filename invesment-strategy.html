Investment Schemes

Types of Investment Schemes


Investors have the option to invest in either new projects or existing ones through a takeover, granting them the flexibility to select the scheme that best aligns with their preferences and objectives.

This freedom empowers investors to make informed decisions tailored to their specific needs and circumstances. By offering a range of investment choices, the market accommodates diverse investor preferences, fostering a more dynamic and adaptable investment landscape.

Project Takeover Scheme

  • Immediate access to existing assets, revenue streams, and market share.
  • Potential for cost synergies and economies of scale.
  • Reduced uncertainty compared to starting a new project.

New Project Scheme

  • Opportunity to pursue innovative ideas and disruptive technologies.
  • Greater control over the project's direction and strategy.
  • Potential for higher long-term returns if successful.

For further insights into investment opportunities tailored to your goals and preferences, we invite you to reach out to our marketing team.

Project Collaboration

Private Sector Projects

Independent projects solely driven by private entities. Private sector takes full responsibility for planning, funding, and execution without government support. Relies on private sector resources, expertise, and market dynamics.

Examples: Corporate investments, private ventures.

B2B Schemes (Business to Business)

Collaborative ventures among businesses. Businesses work together with minimal government backing, focusing on a conducive regulatory environment and fair competition. Private sector leads planning, financing, and execution. Limited or indirect involvement by government.

Examples: Joint ventures, strategic partnerships.

Cooperation Projects (KPBU)

Public-Private Partnership (PPP) initiatives backed by government support. Active government participation and collaboration with private sectors in planning, funding, and implementation. The government provides financial support, guarantees, and assistance to ensure project success.

Examples: Infrastructure and development projects.
