Types of Investment Schemes


Every investment we choose brings more than financial growth, but also a positive impact on society and the environment.

As we think of the younger generation, we feel the responsibility of providing them a promising future. Through the Green Project, we believe we can bring forth necessary changes and spread sustainable awareness to create a promising future that persist through generational changes.


Project Takeover

- Immediate access to existing assets, revenue streams, and market share.
- Potential for cost synergies and economies of scale.
- Reduced uncertainty compared to starting a new project.


New Project

- Opportunity to pursue innovative ideas and disruptive technologies.
- Greater control over the project's direction and strategy.
- Potential for higher long-term returns if successful.


Considerations

Investors should assess their risk tolerance, investment horizon, and strategic objectives when deciding between a takeover and a new project. Diversification across both approaches can mitigate overall portfolio risk and maximize potential returns.

Project Collaboration


Cooperation Projects (KPBU)

- Description: Public-Private Partnership (PPP) initiatives backed by government support.
- Key Features: Active government participation and collaboration with private sectors in planning, funding, and implementation.
- Government Role: Provides financial support, guarantees, and assistance to ensure project success.
- Examples: Infrastructure and development projects.

B2B Schemes (Business-to-Business)

- Description: Collaborative ventures among businesses with limited government intervention.
- Key Features: Businesses work together with minimal government backing, focusing on a conducive regulatory environment and fair competition.
- Private Sector Role: Leads planning, financing, and execution.
- Government Role: Limited or indirect involvement.
- Examples: Joint ventures, strategic partnerships.

Private Sector Projects with No Government Involvement

- Description: Independent projects solely driven by private entities.
- Key Features: Private sector takes full responsibility for planning, funding, and execution without government support.
- Success Factors: Relies on private sector resources, expertise, and market dynamics.
- Examples: Corporate investments, private ventures.
