Energy Efficiency

Energy efficiency initiatives encompass a broad spectrum of strategies aimed at reducing energy consumption and promoting the use of renewable resources across diverse sectors.

These efforts entail adopting advanced technologies and practices to optimize energy utilization, such as integrating renewable energy systems like solar, wind, and micro-hydroelectric projects into operations. Projects that achieve significant reductions, typically around 20% or more, in energy consumption are often prioritized and supported.

In cases where projects fall short of the 20% efficiency improvement benchmark, the development of robust business cases becomes essential. These business cases are instrumental in justifying investments in energy-efficient upgrades, such as transitioning to energy-efficient lighting or implementing Variable Frequency Drives, which offer significant energy savings over time.

Energy efficiency comes in different form, and The Green Project provides EMS as a form of energy efficiency.

Internet of Things (IoT) Rules of Thumb

Energy Management System (EMS) operates within a hierarchical control system comprising four layers based on the Internet of Things (IoT) rules of thumb:

01

Physical Layer

This layer involves direct interaction with the physical world, typically through sensors and actuators.

02

Network Layer

Serving as a bridge between the physical layer and the cloud, this layer commonly includes gateways.

03

Cloud Layer

Connecting the application layer with the network layer, the cloud operates over the internet, enabling users to access the system from anywhere in the world with internet connectivity.

04

Application Layer

This dynamic layer is accessed by users, adapting to their desired conditions in practice.

Through IoT infrastructure, EMS allows users to access energy systems at their respective sites anytime, anywhere, in real-time.

The communication capabilities of EMS vary and are influenced by the network layer used:

True Real-Time

Enables direct and immediate communication with the system.

Non-True Real-Time

Involves data transfer from sensors to the system on a daily basis, updated once a day.
