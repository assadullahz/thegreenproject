Green Energy

Southeast Asia has promising potential in the development of green energy projects. Hydropower, Solar and Biomass are among the promising alternative for sustainable energy that has lucid results in the past. Investing in more projects surrounding green energy is an auspicious move as the market potential continues to grow, and the impact is beyond monetary values. It brings hope that generations to come will be able to enjoy a fulfilling life.

Hydro Power Plant

  • Also known as mini/micro-hydro which is a small-scale power plant that utilizes water as the main source of power
  • The market size of hydropower in Southeast Asia is expectedto reach 52.722 GW by the end of 2023 with a CAGR of 7.9%
  • The Green Projects: Bengkulu (4 MW), Dominanga (7MW)

Solar Power Plant

  • Solar power plants utilizes solar as the main source of electricity generation
  • Southeast Asia has a 16 TW photovoltaic (PV) potential with Myanmar, Indonesia, and Thailand contributing to around 75% of manufacturing capability in Southeast Asia per 2023
  • The Green Projects: Polman (431 kwp), Palu (15 kwp)

Biomass

  • Utilizing organic material such as plants and animals as the main source of energy
  • Bioenergy potential in Indonesia reaches 32.6 GW, but 1.9 GW of bioenergy power plant has been installed per 2020
  • The Green Projects: Chevron Power Plant.
