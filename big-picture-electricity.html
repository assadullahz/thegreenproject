Electricity

Indonesia's Renewable Energy Revolution

Targeting 23% Renewable Energy by 2025 with 6.5 GW Geothermal, 5.4 GW Solar, and 3 GW Wind Capacity

Indonesia is steadfast in its pursuit of a renewable energy future, exemplified by its goal to significantly increase the contribution of clean energy sources to its overall energy mix. With an ambitious target of achieving 23% renewable energy in its total energy consumption by 2025, Indonesia is strategically investing in various sustainable alternatives.

The nation aims to harness 6.5 gigawatts of electricity from geothermal sources, construct 5.4 gigawatts of solar power capacity, and implement 3 gigawatts of wind power projects. This commitment aligns with Indonesia's overarching vision to reduce carbon emissions by 29% by 2030, as outlined in its Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).

By quantifying these targets, Indonesia is not only addressing its energy needs but also positioning itself as a regional exemplar in the global drive towards a low-carbon and environmentally sustainable future.
